On-Time-In-Full (OTIF) Ordering

A Deep Dive into OTIF Ordering Performance

What is On-Time-In-Full Ordering?

On-Time-In-Full (OTIF) Ordering is a critical performance indicator that measures the percentage of purchase order lines processed perfectly. It tells us how effectively we translate planned inventory needs into supplier orders, focusing on two simple but essential questions: was the order placed promptly, and was it for the exact quantity required?

On-Time

An order line is considered "On-Time" if it's reviewed and sent to the supplier within 24 hours of being released by the inventory planning system.

In-Full

An order line is "In-Full" if the quantity ordered from the supplier is exactly equal to the quantity released from the planning system. No more, no less.

The OTIF Evaluation Process

For an order line to achieve a 100% OTIF score, it must successfully pass two critical checks. Any failure along this path results in a 0% score for that specific line. This flow illustrates the all-or-nothing nature of the metric.

How to Influence Your Score

The OTIF score is directly in the hands of the purchasing specialist. Adhering to two simple rules is the key to achieving a high score and driving operational efficiency.

‚úÖ What Helps Your Score

  • Act Fast: Process all new order lines from the planning system within 24 hours.
  • Be Precise: Order the exact quantity that the system has released. Do not make changes.

‚ùå What Hurts Your Score

  • Delaying Orders: Letting an order sit for more than 24 hours before processing it.
  • Changing Quantities: Adjusting the order quantity up or down for any reason.
  • Manual Orders: Adding order lines manually that were not released by the planning system.

Important Exclusions

Certain order types are explicitly excluded from the On-Time-In-Full Ordering calculation for accurate performance measurement.

Order Type Influence on OTIF Score
Standard Planned Orders Directly influences (positively or negatively)
Manually Added Orders (not "Specials") Negatively influences (typically 0% for line)
Special Orders (e.g., promotions, "SPEC%") No influence (excluded)
Direct Shipments No influence (excluded)
Transit Orders No influence (excluded); labeled in OMS as ST lines.
